Biography
Pingchuan Ma (馬平川) is a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate at MIT CSAIL advised by Prof. Wojciech Matusik. Previously, he worked with Prof. Bo Ren and Prof. Ming-Ming Cheng at Nankai University. His research goal covers the whole pipeline of physics-based intelligence: (1) reconstructing efficient realistic physics-based environments, (2) generating intelligent agents upon the environments, and (3) realizing the agents in the physical world. Name

Family name: 馬(Mǎ) means horse.

Given name: 平(Píng) 川(chuān) means flat and level ground without geographical barrier.

Quoted from a Chinese idiom 一(yī) 馬(mǎ) 平(píng) 川(chuān) , my full name means the flat ground that one can ride straight across and thus implies enjoying a smooth life.

眾(zhòng) 峰(fēng) 來(lái) 自(zì) 天(tiān) 目(mù) 山(shān) ， 勢(shì) 若(ruò) 駿(jùn) 馬(mǎ) 奔(bèn) 平(píng) 川(chuān) 。

宋(Sòng) · 蘇(Sū) 軾(Shì) 《 東(Dōng) 坡(pō) 全(quán) 集(jí) · 卷(Juàn) 三(sān) · 遊(Yóu) 徑(jìng) 山(shān) 》

There are lots of mountains from Tianmu Mountain, which have the might of fine horses galloping across flat ground.
