Biography
Pingchuan Ma (馬平川) is a final-year Ph.D. candidate at MIT CSAIL advised by Prof. Wojciech Matusik. Previously, he worked with Prof. Bo Ren and Prof. Ming-Ming Cheng at Nankai University. His research goal covers the whole pipeline of physics-based intelligence: (1) reconstructing efficient realistic physics-based world models, (2) generating intelligent agents upon the world models, and (3) realizing the agents in the physical world. Name

Family name: 馬(Mǎ) means horse.

Given name: 平(Píng)川(chuān) means flat and level ground without geographical barrier.

Quoted from a Chinese idiom 一馬平川, my full name means the flat ground that one can ride straight across and thus implies enjoying a smooth life.

眾峰來自天目山，勢若駿馬奔平川。

宋·蘇軾 《東坡全集·卷三·遊徑山》

There are lots of mountains from Tianmu Mountain, which have the might of fine horses galloping across flat ground.

Su Shi of the Song dynasty in Visit Jing Mountain
